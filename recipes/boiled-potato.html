Boiled Potato(es)

Description

It seems simple - doesn't it? Just some boiled potatoes. Well, you're wrong. This recipe is forged in blood. Generation upon generation have fought to keep this recipe safe. My bloodline have protected this recipe, and now, my squire, I shall pass this knowledge on to you. Anyway, here's the recipe:

Ingredients

  • 1kg Potatoes - any variety you can get your grubby hands on.
  • Unsalted Butter - salted butter is for the poor, the unfortunate, or the stupid. If you're reading this, luckily, you're none of these.
  • Extra Virgin Olive Oil - yes, you read that right. Just one fat is not enough. To truly experience the potatoes the way the gods do, we need to go further.
  • Salt - a potato without salt is akin to an angel without its wings.
  • Black Pepper - the dynamic duo. Whilst salt shines in the spotlight, black pepper's always there doing the REAL work to make sure the forces of magic are in balance.
  • Parsley - ah, the ingredient to truly separate these potatoes from those of the common-folk. If you do not like it, I'm sorry to say, but nothing great shall ever come of you.

Steps

  1. Wash and scrub potatoes and cut larger potatoes in halves or even quarters so the potato pieces are even-sized.
  2. Place potatoes in a pot of water with 15g of salt and cover with 2.6cm of water (Not a drop more or fewer, lest you're ready to face the consequences). Bring to a boil then reduce heat and simmer until potatoes are fork tender (10-18 minutes depending on size).
  3. Drain potatoes and return them to the pot, or a mixing bowl.
  4. Drizzle melted butter (figure out how to melt it yourself - it's part of the task) and olive oil over potatoes.
  5. Season to taste with salt and black pepper.
  6. Garnish with a feeshly chopped parsley and toss to combine. Keep covered until ready to serve.
